Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, a pioneer of peace and non-violence, continues to inspire millions worldwide.

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, was a leader of India's independence movement against British rule. Known as the "Father of the Nation," Gandhi's principles of non-violence and truth (Satyagraha) remain a guiding light for civil rights movements globally.

He led significant campaigns like the Salt March and advocated for religious harmony, social justice, and the upliftment of the underprivileged. Gandhi's legacy is a testament to the power of peaceful protest.

Why I Admire Mahatma Gandhi

I admire Mahatma Gandhi for his unwavering commitment to truth, non-violence, and justice. His courage to stand against oppression with peaceful resistance inspires me to pursue equality and harmony in my own life. His words, "Be the change you wish to see in the world," resonate deeply.
